## 2. Structure and Style (All Example Papers vs. Ours)

| Element | JEEM papers (1–5) | Adjacent (6–8) | Our working paper |
|--------|--------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Abstract** | One paragraph; some have highlights; JEL + keywords | Structured or one para; keywords | Structured (Purpose, Theory, Method, Findings, Implications) + Keywords ✓ |
| **Highlights** | Semrau has 5 bullets | Wang (JEM) has none; Zhang (JBR) none | Missing → **Add 4–5 highlights** |
| **JEL** | All have 2–4 codes | Lu has; Wang/Zhang not always | Missing → **Add JEL** |
| **Introduction** | Compact: problem, gap, contribution, roadmap | Similar | Longer (hook + lit + 3 gaps + RQ + 3 contributions + preview) ✓ |
| **Literature** | Often Section 2 “Literature” or “Background” + integrated in intro | Standalone or in intro | Standalone Related Literature ✓ |
| **Theory / Hypotheses** | Clear H1, H2… with one sentence each; mechanism stated | Same | Same (H1–H5 in quote blocks) ✓ |
| **Methods** | One “Data”/“Methodology”/“Empirical strategy” section; subsections for data, variables, specification | Same | Separate Methods (design, sample, variables, analysis, endogeneity, validity) ✓ |
| **Results** | Main tables → robustness; coefficients + SE; often % or elasticity interpretation | Same | Placeholders; N, K, years filled ✓ |
| **Discussion** | Often brief; policy in conclusion | Wang/Zhang have discussion + implications | Full Discussion (summary, theory, implications, limitations) ✓ |
| **Conclusion** | Short; policy + future research | Same | Short Conclusion ✓ |

**Implication:** Add **Highlights** and **JEL** to match JEEM. Keep current structure; optional slight tightening of Introduction.

## 3. Theory and Hypotheses

| Paper | Theory | Hypotheses | Our comparison |
|-------|--------|------------|----------------|
| Wang et al. JEEM 2018 | Regulation → emissions → productivity; emission as input | Implicit (regulation effect on COD, then on TFP) | We: digitalization → TFP and env; regulation as moderator |
| Shi & Xu JEEM 2018 | PHE; policy variation | Stricter regulation → less export in dirty industries | We: regulation moderates digitalization–env link |
| Benatti et al. JEEM 2024 | Porter (strong/weak/narrow); endogeneity review | Policy tightening → TFP growth (test PH) | We: double dividend; regulation amplifies digitalization–env |
| Albrizio et al. JEEM 2017 | Neo-Schumpeterian; PH; frontier distance | EPS → MFP growth; heterogeneity by frontier, policy type | We: heterogeneity by regulation, ownership, size |
| Semrau JEEM 2026 | GVC, scale–composition–technique; learning-by-exporting; regulation-push | H1–H4: upstreamness → CO2; export and EPS moderate | We: H1–H5 main + mediation + moderation |
| Lu & Pless | Selection, reallocation, within-firm upgrading | Heterogeneous DiD; mechanisms | We: mechanisms (green innovation); heterogeneity |
| Wang et al. JEM 2026 | Digital economy → green development; green tech + green management innovation | Mechanisms + moderators (attention, competition, digital exec) | **Closest to us:** digital → green; mechanisms; moderators ✓ |
| Zhang et al. JBR 2026 | Institutional theory (coercive, mimetic, normative) | Digitalization → supplier env; institutional pressure moderates | We use institutional theory; regulation as moderator ✓ |

**Implication:** Our theory (institutional + technology-adoption) and H1–H5 align with JEEM and with Wang (JEM) and Zhang (JBR). Emphasize in Intro/Related Lit that we **combine** digitalization–environment (Wang, Zhang) with **regulation–productivity** (JEEM) and **double dividend** in heavy-pollution firms.

## 5. Identification and Endogeneity

| Paper | Baseline | IV / design | Our comparison |
|-------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Wang et al. JEEM 2018 | Regulation on COD; production function | Regulation timing + designation | We: FE + lagged digitalization; IV (historical comm., peer digitalization) |
| Shi & Xu JEEM 2018 | DDD | IV: ventilation coefficient for provincial target | We: FE; IV planned |
| Benatti et al. JEEM 2024 | Local projections; exposure by emissions | Policy stringency (country-level); firm exposure | We: firm FE; regulation as moderator |
| Albrizio et al. JEEM 2017 | Industry + firm; frontier distance | EPS index (country); heterogeneity | We: firm FE; industry/region |
| Semrau JEEM 2026 | OLS; no firm FE (upstreamness slow-moving) | IV for upstreamness (Section 5.1) | We: **firm FE** (digitalization time-varying); state **explicit identification** paragraph |
| Lu & Pless | Heterogeneous DiD | Prefecture×time×industry intensity | We: within-firm variation; lag; IV |
| Wang et al. JEM 2026 | Panel FE | Mechanisms + moderators | We: same spirit ✓ |
| Zhang et al. JBR 2026 | Two-way cluster SE; dyadic FE | Institutional moderators | We: firm FE; cluster SE; IV for digitalization |

**Implication:** Add a short **Identification** subsection in Methods: “We rely on within-firm variation in digitalization and outcomes over time, controlling for time-invariant firm heterogeneity and common shocks. Lagged digitalization reduces reverse causality. In IV specifications, we use …” (mirror Semrau’s explicit rationale for no firm FE / cross-section).

## 7. Synthesis: Common Patterns Across Example Papers

1. **Front matter:** JEL + Keywords (all JEEM); Highlights (Semrau). → **Add JEL and Highlights.**
2. **Theory:** Clear H1, H2…; mechanism stated. → We already do; can add one sentence per H on mechanism.
3. **Data:** Precise source and period; variable construction explicit. → **Add exact formulas and open-data statement.**
4. **Identification:** Either explicit “no firm FE because…” (Semrau) or “within-firm + lag + IV” (us). → **Add Identification paragraph.**
5. **Results:** Real coefficients, SE, and **economic interpretation** (%, elasticity). → **Replace placeholders; add % effects.**
6. **Policy:** All have policy implications (brief in JEEM, longer in JEM/JBR). → We have full Discussion ✓.

## 8. Recommendations for the Working Paper (Revised)

1. **Abstract & front:** Keep 5-part abstract + Keywords. **Add Highlights (4–5 bullets)** and **JEL codes** (e.g. O44, Q52, Q53, Q55, L25).
2. **Methods:** Add **Identification** subsection (within-firm variation, lag, IV). For each main variable, add **one sentence on construction and source** (e.g. digitalization: “ln(1 + count of digital keywords) from CNINFO annual reports”).
3. **Results:** Replace placeholders with **real coefficients, SE, p-values**; report **economic magnitude** (e.g. one-SD increase in digitalization → X% change in TFP / env. performance). Consider **descriptive figure** (e.g. mean TFP or env. by year).
4. **Data/reproducibility:** State clearly that **all data are open**; list sources (AKShare, CNINFO, CNIPA, NBS, MEE) and, if applicable, code/repo URL.
5. **Discussion:** Add one sentence **contrasting** our setting (digitalization, China, double dividend, heavy-pollution) with **GVC/upstreamness** (Semrau) and **regulation-only** (Wang/Shi/Benatti/Albrizio) to sharpen contribution.
6. **Related Literature:** Optionally cite **Wang JEM 2026** and **Zhang JBR 2026** explicitly in Related Lit as closest to our digitalization–environment–mechanisms–moderators setup.
