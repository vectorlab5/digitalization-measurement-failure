# Figures and Tables: Example Papers vs. Our Working Paper

This document compares **figures and tables** across the example papers and our manuscript, and lists **what to add** so we align with JEEM and adjacent journals.

---

## 1. What Each Example Paper Has

### Wang et al. (JEEM 2018) — COD, emissions, TFP, China
| Item | Content |
|------|--------|
| **Table 1** | Policy background: key regions designation (basin, date, industry codes) |
| **Table 2** | Regulated industries by region and period |
| **Table 3** | Industrial COD emissions: mean levels and annual growth (3Rs3Ls vs non-3Rs3Ls) |
| **Table 4** | Matched data: by year — # firms, gross output, capital, employment, intermediates, COD, COD intensity |
| **Table 5** | One-year difference: mean growth rates (output, capital, employment, intermediates, COD) by period |
| **Table 6** | Main regression: effect of water quality regulation on TFP (5 columns: different FE specs + interaction) |
| **Tables 7+** | Emission function; production function; robustness (appendix) |
| **Figures** | None in main text (conceptual in text) |

### Shi & Xu (JEEM 2018) — Regulation, exports, China
| Item | Content |
|------|--------|
| **Table 2** | OLS: impact of regulation on exports (2 cols: proportion of exporters, Ln(export)) |
| **Table 3** | IV: first stage (2 cols) + second stage (2 cols); F-test reported |
| **Table 4** | Pre-existing time trends: year-by-year triple interaction (identification check) |
| **Table 5** | Robustness: concurrent events (VAT reform, crisis, Olympics); alternative samples |
| **Figures** | None in main text |

|------|--------|

### Albrizio et al. (JEEM 2017) — EPS, MFP, OECD

|------|--------|

### Semrau (JEEM 2026) — Upstreamness, CO2, India
| Item | Content |
|------|--------|
| **Table 1** | **Summary statistics:** all variables — N, Mean, SD, Min, Max (full variable list) |
| **Table 2** | **Industry breakdown:** CO2, CO2/SA, CO2/VA, upstreamness by industry (ordered by upstreamness) |
| **Fig. 1** | **Descriptive:** Mean and median CO2 (and CO2/SA, CO2/VA) over time (6 panels) |
| **Table 3** | **Main regression:** OLS upstreamness → CO2 (6 columns: 3 DVs × with/without industry FE) |
| **Fig. 2** | **Margins:** Predicted ln(CO2), ln(CO2/SA), ln(CO2/VA) by upstreamness percentiles (3 panels) |
| **Table 4** | **Moderation:** Export status (cols 1–3) and EPS (cols 4–6) interaction with upstreamness |
| **Fig. 3** | **Interaction plot:** CO2/VA by upstreamness at different EPS levels; by EPS at different upstreamness |

## 4. Recommended Priority

**High priority (must have for JEEM-style submission)**  
1. **Expand Table 1** — Full descriptives (Mean, SD, Min, Max; N, K); fill TFP, env, digitalization when ready.  
2. **Table 2 — Main regression** — At least 4 columns (TFP baseline, TFP + dig; Env baseline, Env + dig); coefficients (SE), stars, N, R².

4. **Table 4 — Moderation** — Regulation interaction (H4); optionally H5 (ownership/size).  
5. **Figure 1 — Conceptual model** — Proper diagram (draw.io or TikZ), not text-in-box.

6. **Table 5 — Robustness** — Alternative specs; **IV table** (first stage + second stage).  
7. **Figure 2 — Descriptive** — Mean TFP or env. perf. or digitalization by year.  

**Lower priority (nice to have)**

9. **Figure 4 — Interaction** (moderation).  
10. **Industry breakdown table** (by sector or industry).
