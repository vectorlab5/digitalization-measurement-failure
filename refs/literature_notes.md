## [Wen et al.] (2021) – Digitalization and environment: how does ICT affect enterprise environmental performance?

**Journal**: Environmental Science and Pollution Research, 28, 54826–54841.

**Core finding**: Industrial digitalization improves environmental performance of Chinese manufacturing firms; structural and technology effects are channels; structural effect is main contributor. Digitalization reduces heavy-polluting scale, improves green TFP; **insignificant effect on (conventional) TFP**.

**Theory**: ICT / industrial digitalization.

**Method**: Large sample Chinese manufacturing; transmission channels.

**Link to our study**: Direct precedent for “digitalization → environmental performance”; we add **TFP** and **heavily polluting firms** focus, **mediation** (green innovation), **moderation** (regulation). Cite in Theory and Related work.

**BibTeX key**: `wen2021digitalization`

## [DiMaggio & Powell] (1983) – Iron cage revisited

**Journal**: American Sociological Review.

**Core finding**: Institutional isomorphism: organizations conform to environmental expectations for legitimacy.

**Link to our study**: Theory for “environmental regulation → firm behavior” and legitimacy motive for green/digital adoption. Cite in Theory (§3.4).

**BibTeX key**: `dimaggio1983iron`

## [Porter & Van der Linde] (1995) – Environment-competitiveness

**Journal**: Journal of Economic Perspectives.

**Core finding**: Porter hypothesis: well-designed regulation can induce innovation and improve competitiveness.

**Link to our study**: Motivation for H4 (regulation moderates digitalization–environment relationship). Cite in Theory and Discussion.

**BibTeX key**: `porter1995green`
