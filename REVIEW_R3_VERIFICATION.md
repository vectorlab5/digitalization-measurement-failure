# Third-Round Review: Structural Conversion to Measurement-Transparency Paper

**Reviewer verdict:** "Reject, but the manuscript now has a coherent alternative identity as a measurement-transparency paper." The paper had completed a *content* shift but not a *structural* one: "the structure of the paper still resembles an empirical study, while the content explicitly acknowledges that the empirical design cannot identify the theoretical constructs."

---

## Checklist of structural changes implemented

| Reviewer requirement | Implementation |
|----------------------|----------------|
| **1. Delete H1–H5** | Theory section retitled "Conceptual Framework"; all numbered hypotheses (H1–H5) removed. Framework presents direct effects, mediation, moderation as conceptual links only. No "Hypothesis 1 (H1):" or testable predictions. |
| **2. Rewrite Results title** | Section retitled *"Illustration: What Happens When Revenue-Based Proxies Replace Constructs"*. |
| **3. Remove "Main results"** | All subsection and table language uses "Proxy behavior," "Illustration," "proxy mediator"; no "Main results" or hypothesis-testing claims. |
| **4. Rewrite Introduction ending** | Intro now states we show *why* the double-dividend question is *often untestable* with common open data; contributions are conceptual framework, measurement pitfalls, and blueprint. No promise to "answer" the substantive question. |
| **5. Title change** | New title: *"Measurement Pitfalls in Studying Enterprise Digitalization and Environmental Performance: A Framework and Proxy Illustration from Heavily Polluting Firms"*. |

---

## Additional alignments

- **Abstract:** Opens with measurement pitfalls and "why the question is often untestable"; no hypothesis-testing claim.
- **Highlights:** All five bullets reframed around measurement-transparency contribution, proxy illustration, and blueprint.
- **Figure 1 caption:** States the figure summarizes the *framework* and that "this paper does not test these links."
- **Tables 2–4:** Captions and notes explicitly say "Illustration," "Not a test of the conceptual framework," "Conceptual mediation ... is not tested," "Year is a time trend, not environmental regulation."
- **Discussion / Conclusion / Related literature / Methods:** All references to "H1–H5" or "hypotheses" removed; replaced with "conceptual framework," "proxy illustration," "target constructs."

---

## Resulting identity

The manuscript is now structured as a **measurement-transparency / methodological note**: it defines the research question, presents a conceptual framework (without formal hypotheses), documents target vs. implemented design, and illustrates proxy behavior to show why the question is untestable with the implemented proxies. Tables and Results are explicitly "illustrations," not "main results." This removes the empirical-paper shell vs. methodological-paper content conflict identified in the third-round review.
